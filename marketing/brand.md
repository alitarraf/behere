# BeHereNow — Brand

**Tagline:** An interruption you never see coming.

## What it is (one line)
A bell. A few times a day, at moments no one chooses, it rings — sometimes
only a buzz, sometimes a single line, sometimes a shape that lives ten
seconds and is gone forever. No history, no streaks, no accounts.

## Voice
Terse, unhurried, a little koan-shaped. Field notes from building a small,
strange, personal instrument — never marketing copy for a wellness product.

**Never say:** mindfulness, meditation, self-care, journey, wellness,
"improve your focus," "reduce stress," or anything that promises an
outcome. This app makes no claims and tracks no results — that's the whole
point. Posts should describe the mechanic plainly (the randomness, the
three forms the bell takes, the fact that nothing is ever saved) and let
the philosophy stay implicit, the same way the app itself never explains
itself on the screen.

**Grounding reference (use sparingly, don't over-explain it):** built after
Krishnamurti, Tolle, Osho, Ram Dass, and the Record of Linji — specifically
the three-blows story, where Huangbo strikes the young monk Linji instead
of answering his question. The teaching was never punishment; it was that
presence can't be explained, only interrupted into. This app's own line:
"an interruption you can see coming is just another appointment."

be here now

This is a bell.

A few times a day, at moments no one chooses, it rings — sometimes only a buzz, sometimes a single line, sometimes ten seconds of something that will never exist again.

It keeps no history. No streaks, no progress, nothing to review, nothing to browse. It holds no memory of you, and gives you nothing to hold onto either.

Either you meet the moment, or it passes. Both are fine. Another will come.

Twelve centuries ago the young monk Linji asked his master Huangbo, "What is the essential meaning of the Buddha's teaching?" Before he finished, Huangbo struck him. He asked twice more, and was struck twice more.

Defeated, Linji went to the hermit Dayu and told his story. "Huangbo exhausted himself for your sake, kind as a grandmother," said Dayu, "and you come asking what you did wrong?" At those words Linji woke up — and became the master whose own school taught with a shout that cut thought mid-sentence.

The blows were never punishment. They were the teaching itself: presence cannot be explained, only interrupted into. And an interruption you can see coming is just another appointment.

This is a small stick that lives in your pocket.

after Krishnamurti · Tolle · Osho · Ram Dass · and the Record of Linji

To let it ring, it has to live on the home screen:

  1. Chrome menu (⋮) → Add to Home screen → Install
  2. Open it from the home screen icon — not this tab
  3. Tap begin, allow notifications
If the bell arrives silently (Samsung)
  1. Long-press an arrived notification → switch Silent to Alert
  2. Or: Settings → Apps → BeHereNow → Notifications → allow sound & vibration (categories hide behind Settings → Notifications → Advanced → "Manage notification categories for each app")
  3. Check the notification volume slider — it is separate from ring volume
iPhone (later)

Needs iOS 16.4+. Share sheet → Add to Home Screen first — iOS only allows notifications for installed web apps — then open from the icon and tap begin.
